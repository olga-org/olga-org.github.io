### I. Origins & Founder’s Background

**Olga:** Karolina, you come from social sciences and NGOs—what was the ‘Aha!’ moment that made you realize your strengths were vital in an AI-tech environment?

Karolina Budzisz: First, just to clarify – I am not the startup’s founder. My current role is Chief of Staff and Community Development, and I started in the company as a Community Developer. The ‘Aha!’ moment you are talking about – that a person with my background can be useful in an AI/tech environment – was experienced rather by our CEO, whom I was lucky to be headhunted by.

[Jarek](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jaroslaw-kulacz/), the guy in question, is a person with a mixed background and interests – both in technology and business, but also – passionate about more ‘social’ themes and human interactions. When building [joinExpertHub](https://www.joinexperthub.com/), he was guided by the idea of a platform that would constitute an online space for a community of ICT and AI Experts – often fragmented and working independently. This community, he thought, would offer them the opportunities to benefit from social aspects, normally missing from their work. And it had to be managed by a person who has experience working with people rather than machines.

His vision was to build a human-friendly place where freelancers and startup founders can feel the spirit of togetherness and healthy competition. My project management experience in the non-governmental sector, linked with some administrative skills and passion for facilitating human connections, seemed to be a good fit to support this idea.

My role evolved on the way, and extended to building community outside of joinExpertHub, i.e., gaining potential partners, networking, creating content interesting for our stakeholders, and managing internal team’s encounters, alongside Jarek.

Nurturing a community, setting up a network, planning workshops, events, initiatives, and content – these tasks occur equally much in both the grass-roots level organizations and tech-startup ecosystem. The tools and techniques remain the same, but what is different is the substance that is being talked about. That is the area that I am currently developing my knowledge in, and I must say – it is exciting to learn about innovation and be surrounded by innovative minds!

We think that this social approach, combined with the tech skills of other members of joinExpertHub’s team, can strengthen our potential to succeed and highlight our values and integrity.

### II. The Idea & Innovation

**Olga:** [SolveStack.ai](http://SolveStack.ai) aims to solve the ‘AI cold-start’ problem—what was the human insight behind choosing a matchmaking algorithm over pure human curation?

**Karolina:** We believe that a vast, global AI implementation is inevitable. We are not able to avoid it, and therefore, it is high time to embrace it and learn about it as much as we can. We are a company that wants to stay up-to-date with the newest technologies, understand our community, our clients, and the realities of the industry.

Choosing our focus to be directed on AI, we ought to know how it functions and simply side with it. If we want to promote AI solutions, we need to believe AI solutions are good. And there is no better way to prove it than applying it ourselves.

We believe AI, just like any technology, should make human life easier but not replace tasks that require a truly human approach.

As you noticed, in our case, AI facilitates data management and matching process. It makes things go smoother and faster – it screens, sifts out the mismatches and prevents errors that we as humans are more prone to.

But once AI offers an automated suggestion that our team can take a look at, we take over right there. Once a startup or an expert is matched with a client enterprise – it is our ’human resources’ who oversee the project implementation and management.

Our newest component, [SolveStack.ai](http://SolveStack.ai), a platform matching AI-driven startups with enterprises that seek AI solutions, will be launched in the third quarter of 2025.

The idea behind it stems from previous experiences of our team – having worked both at large corporations and in small startup environments. What we are sure of is that startups and enterprises often speak different languages, work at different paces, and simply exist in separate commercial universes.

There is a clear correlation emerging in the current economy – companies willing to implement AI projects, and startups offering more and more of those. So, where is the problem? In finding each other, trusting each other, and sourcing guidance from the experts with the right know-how.

So, here we are. Introducing enterprises to startups and allowing them to grow their projects effectively, additionally sourcing external Experts whenever needed.

### III. Relevance of Finnish Startup Job Market & Talents

**Olga:** With Finland’s high-trust culture (esim. sisu), how do you see Finnish AI talent standing out in SolveStack matches?

**Karolina:** We don’t limit ourselves to the Finnish startup and tech talent ecosystems. Of course, we are willing to discover the broader potential within the country, the innovation within and the team’s connotation to it. But we are hoping to become a hub for a broader range of European startups and for global experts and clients.

We can, however, distinguish some trends in Finland that make us believe it is a good target direction. What speaks to us the most is the Finnish way of working, trust-based, balanced, and often impact-driven. It makes the Finnish companies and talent stand out – especially in a commercial and technological race, where the values are commonly lost on the way to success. Resilience (sisu), so ubiquitous in Finland, is simply THE feature of a good entrepreneur and innovator.

On top of that, onboarding Finnish startups and talent gives more opportunities to our company – as the Nordic inventions have a well-established reputation. But what would the Finnish experts and solution-providers gain from cooperating with us?

On multiple occasions, we heard from the Finnish founders, that it is incredibly difficult to grow meaningfully in a market so relatively small. JoinExpertHub, and particularly the [SolveStack.ai](http://SolveStack.ai) component, will provide Finnish AI entrepreneurs and talent with an opportunity to scale and test the applicability of their projects worldwide, interact with international colleagues and export the Finnish solutions abroad.

Moreover, our team offers facilitation of the connection with clients. Even though there are plenty of tech specialists, the startups often lack people who know how to connect to clients, support the GTM (Go-To-Market) strategies, or communicate effectively across stakeholders. This would be one area that we can offer our support as well.

### IV. Tech, AI, Data & Ethics

**Olga:** As AI accelerates, emotional intelligence often lags—is there a moment you’ve inserted an ethical or human concern into SolveStack’s pipeline?

**Karolina:** One of the steps in the process of onboarding startups is the encounter with a team behind the ideas. We emphasize the importance of screening companies that we will vouch for in the future – not only in regards of their technology but also value alignment with joinExpertHub. We realized that by trusting us, the clients will trust the companies in our database, and that puts a lot of responsibility on us to sift out misaligning entrepreneurs and use-cases.

Besides, ethics and values are behind the idea of opening community development department in the first place. We have designed community rules that we update regularly, where we state that in our space, there is no room for aggression and discrimination. Moreover, the encouragement of ethical AI use can be seen in the discourse of our content, including the community blog available on our Pinboard.

Moreover, our team works on the AI for Good initiatives – promoting actions and startups which may not fit in our commercial base, but which operationalize AI for a good social cause, e.g. in parenting or accessible education.

Even though we are aware that the AI algorithms may be biased – the factors that create the basis for the match-making happening on our platform are purely tech-related. Even though, due to market reasons, we focus on onboarding EU-based startups, we recruit experts globally, assessing their potential for the project based on their skill and experience. As a company driven majorly by women, we are also willing to empower female experts joining the platform, but we do not address any of our activities exclusively to one of the genders.

Lastly, we are also happy to work with diverse clients to learn the global pain points and the ways they can be soothed.

### V. Vision & Future

**Olga:** Five years out, how would joinExpertHub’s community culture need to evolve to support your vision of a human‑centered AI ecosystem?

**Karolina:** We believe human-native intervention is essential in facilitating the connection between various actors. And this is our main task at joinExpertHub. Even though we are supported by an algorithm and we are existing in a tech-driven environment, only people can fully empathize with problems that require tech-driven solutions.

It does not mean that AI cannot support humans in doing so. The cooperation of technology and people is essential to dynamize work, make it more effective and innovative. But it is human intelligence that needs to oversee it and direct the process.

Our community development direction is adjusting to the needs of our experts and solution-providers on the go. We are willing to get to know them better, to be able to understand their challenges and support them in overcoming those. We will offer more and more of what is being needed by them – as long as it aligns with the company’s values.

For instance, we are now becoming more and more diverse in our media content forms, planning to organize events, as well as to facilitate learning and support exchange directly. We strive to show the good, the fun, and the useful sides of AI – giving both our audiences and us more hope for the future, where technology is used securely and assists rather than replaces people.

We hope that in 5 years we will be a recognizable network of AI entrepreneurs and talents, where the new tech enthusiasts can source advice, knowledge, opportunities, and connections from. We strive for the community to become ultimately self-driven, with its members shaping it actively.

But… we will then need more and more human hands to succeed with that, even in the AI-oriented climate. :-)
