Exiting meetings on Microsoft Ignite The Tour Milan, Italy (27-28 January 2020)

Created on 2020-02-02

Published on 2020-02-02

This was my first conference abroad in which I participated as a subject matter expert and specialist (MCT) in training users of Microsoft Office 365 and Microsoft Teams. But let's take things in order.

Microsoft Ignite is a conference for developers and IT professionals hosted by Microsoft. The event takes place annually in several cities around the world. The first conference, then called TechEd, took place in 1993 in Orlando, Florida, USA, and the last one was held in Barcelona in 2014. Microsoft changed the format of the conference and in 2015 it became known as Microsoft Ignite. This conference has never been held in Russia before. The conference typically lasts two to five days and consists of talks, presentations, demonstrations, hands-on labs, and "Ask the Expert" sessions. There is an opportunity to personally meet with Microsoft specialists, MCTs, MVPs and active members of the community. Events also include exhibition areas where you can try out the latest technologies and products. There is an extensive catalogue of content from which visitors can choose the most useful sessions for themselves and create a schedule in the application Microsoft Events. The agenda for all days of the conference is published online before the start of the conference on the website Microsoft Ignite.

I was lucky enough to participate in this event at the invitation of Microsoft, sharing my experience in the Ad Hoc Expert Connect conference section. My sessions took place over 2 days, totalling 6 hours. Mostly local Italian representatives of companies signed up for a meeting with me, who wanted to know, for example, about my experience in corporate training, ask specific questions about working with applications, and get an explanation of how they can use Microsoft Teams to organize teamwork in their companies. In addition to participating in the Expert Connect section, I had the opportunity to attend expert sessions, meet them in person and receive first-hand information. I have compiled my TOP 5 most interesting reports that I attended and learned a lot of useful things from.

3. Patrick Guimonet, MVP, Enterprise Solutions Architect

This French specialist had already visited Moscow with his report as part of the aOS Moscow meetup in July last year, which took place at the Microsoft office in Krylatsky Hills, so IT-Pro Russia had the opportunity to get acquainted with his materials and experience in integrating Power Platform with SharePoint and Teams.

4. Mirko Colemberg, MVP - "Why and how you should use Microsoft Insider Programs to get ahead of the next Update"

In this talk, the expert explained why participating in Insider programs is worth it and the strategic benefits companies gain by participating in Insider testing. Previously, only developers were allowed to test; now new versions of the software are available to a wide range of users at an early stage.

5. Adam Deltinger, MVP - "5 Microsoft Teams tips in 15 minutes"

My top five is completed by a blitz report on 5 useful features of MS Teams, which the speaker demonstrated:

  • Creation of closed channels
  • Channel moderation, which allows you to manage the content posted on the channel and indicate whether responses to the publication are accepted
  • Targeted messaging allows team members to send messages to all employees with a specific role at once. To do this, you need to use @ to mention the role name in the post
  • Priority status notifications notify the recipient of an urgent message. Until the recipient responds, a request will be sent to their mobile device or computer every two minutes
  • Apply bookmarks to messages or documents that are important or need to be worked on later

A complete list of reports can be found on the website TechCommunity.

This concludes this brief overview of the useful reports from Microsoft Ignite The Tour Milan. I would like to note that in general, the atmosphere of the conference was very friendly, welcoming and comfortable. I definitely want to attend this event again, but perhaps in another city.
