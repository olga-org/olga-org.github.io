From Classroom to Slush Stage: This Teen Founder Wants to Fix Education with AI — And He’s Starting from Kosovo

Created on 2026-01-04 14:08

Published on 2026-01-05 15:00

I. Origins & CEO’s Background / Founding Story

Olga K. : You reportedly founded MindLoop at a very young age. What childhood hobbies or early curiosities led you toward AI-driven learning, and how did you carry that vision into a startup?

Kaon Krasniqi: I always wanted to build something in an impactful sector like education. When large language models (LLM) became a major force in today’s world, I told myself that this was the right moment to build a real solution for personalized learning.

Olga: When you started MindLoop, did you ever worry that your young age might affect your credibility? How did you overcome doubts, your own or others’?

Kaon: I believe that when you do something you truly love, it doesn’t really matter if obstacles slow you down along the way. You should still continue. I’m trying to solve one of the biggest problems young people face today, personalized learning. That purpose motivates me regardless of age. In other words, my passion for the problem made it much easier to overcome doubts.

Olga: Building a startup so early often accelerates personal and professional growth. How has this shaped your view on education, opportunity, and responsibility?

Kaon: Being a founder completely shifted my perspective on education. Instead of only talking about problems, I now constantly try to build solutions. To me, opportunity means giving individuals or groups the chance to unlock their potential and create real value for the world.

Olga: Who were the first people you brought on board, and what qualities did you look for beyond technical skills?

Kaon: So far, I’m a solo founder. In the near future, I’d love to answer this question again with a team behind me.

Olga: If MindLoop had an animal symbol, what would it be and why?

Kaon: A penguin. Penguins are highly intelligent animals with strong problem-solving skills and exceptional navigation abilities. That resonates deeply with MindLoop’s mission, to help people not just memorize content, but truly master topics and apply what they’ve learned in real-world situations.

II. Idea & Innovation Behind MindLoop

Olga: Many platforms claim “personalized learning.” What makes MindLoop fundamentally different?

Kaon: Most AI tools in today’s market focus on fast learning or quick answers, but not on true mastery of a field or topic. MindLoop is built around helping learners deeply understand and apply knowledge, not just consume information.

III. Relevance of MindLoop & the European Innovation Ecosystem

Olga: How does building from Kosovo shape MindLoop differently compared to a traditional tech hub?

Kaon: What I’m building directly impacts me as well. I’m also a student. I experience the problems I’m trying to solve every day, which gives MindLoop a very authentic, user-driven foundation.

Olga: Do you see MindLoop as part of a broader wave of innovation from Southeastern Europe or the Balkans?

Kaon: I see MindLoop being used globally. While infrastructure can be a challenge for AI-driven education in the region, the optimism and talent here are enormous.

Olga: European education systems can be slow to adopt new technology. How do you plan to navigate this?

Kaon: That hesitation is understandable. AI represents a significant change. I believe adoption will come through time, proof, and evidence by showing institutions real data, real progress, and real impact.

Olga: What could European governments or institutions do to better support young AI founders from places like Kosovo?

Kaon: Offering programs that give talented builders from around the world a chance to showcase their projects at events attended by influential people in technology, would make a huge difference.

Olga: If MindLoop succeeds globally, what impact could that have on perceptions of Kosovo or the Balkans?

Kaon: My goal is to inspire young people from small countries. That even if you come from a small place, you can still make a big impact on the world.

IV. Tech, AI, Data & Ethics

Olga: How do you approach data privacy and ethics, especially for young learners?

Kaon: That depends on the type of partnership we form and the specific requirements of each institution. Privacy and ethical use of data will always be aligned with those standards.

Olga: Do you see AI learning platforms replacing teachers, or working with them?

Kaon: I strongly believe MindLoop will make teachers more effective. AI should support educators, not replace them, by giving them better tools and freeing time for meaningful teaching.

V. Vision & Future of MindLoop

Olga: Five years from now, what does success look like for MindLoop?

Kaon: Success would mean seeing millions of students around the world gaining real-world skills, skills that genuinely shape their careers and futures.

Olga: Could MindLoop expand beyond traditional academic learning?

Kaon: Absolutely. MindLoop is designed to support both hard skills and soft skills.

Olga: How can MindLoop contribute to a more equitable world?

Kaon: By bridging educational gaps and offering an accessible platform where young people can learn the topics they care about and develop the skills they need.
