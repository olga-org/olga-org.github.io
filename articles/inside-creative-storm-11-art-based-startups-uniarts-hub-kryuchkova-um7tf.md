# [Inside the Creative Storm: 11 Art-Based Startups That Redefining Innovation at Uniarts Hub Demo Day](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/inside-creative-storm-11-art-based-startups-uniarts-hub-kryuchkova-um7tf)

*Published on 27-11-2025, 4 min read*

It was inspiring experience to be part of Demo Day — the culmination of this autumn’s pre-incubator journey with Uniarts Hub, based at [**University of the Arts Helsinki**](https://www.linkedin.com/school/uniartshelsinki/). Unique opportunity to witness eleven emerging art-based startups pitch their visions, each bringing something deeply personal, creative and full of promise.

Uniarts Hub’s mission — to help artists and creatives explore entrepreneurship and transform ideas into opportunities — feels more vital than ever. Through structured mentoring, peer support, and business-skill development, the [programme](https://www.uniarts.fi/en/campaigns/develop-your-arts-based-business-idea-in-our-pre-incubator) creates a safe, encouraging space where art meets enterprise.

The competition was fierce. Yet, what struck the most was the shared spirit: boldness, authenticity, and a willingness to grow. The jury represented by Marjo Kenttälä (Business Helsinki), Leena Janhila (Humak University of Applied Sciences), and Aki Pöntinen (ODDfest)— noticed the high quality of the projects and suggested specific ways to improve and support emerging startups further.

Here’s a snapshot of the eleven inspiring projects that made the final stage:

-   Sirja Puurtinen — A deeply experienced artist whose work spans music, performance and media-art. Her background and sensitivity to art’s role in human experience make her project especially compelling.
    
-   Cleide Prado Tommila (she/her) **/ “Colors Without Borders”** — A beautiful cross-cultural initiative weaving together Brazilian and Finnish art through collaborative workshops, offering participants a chance to co-create and discover new artistic dialogues.
    
-   **Siim Maaten / Project Teatteri.in**— An idea to map theatrical performances and help theatre-goers navigate Finland’s rich live-performance landscape with ease — a bridge between audience and art; a clever way to boost accessibility and participation.
    
-   Analía Capponi-Savolainen — A visionary approach to integrate singing into education, offering teachers tools and methods to enrich learning through voice and music — blending pedagogy, art, and human connection.
    
-   Pegah Shamloo **/ Rostan Studio** — A modern ceramics project using 3D-printing technology to create art pieces grounded in everyday life — merging tradition, innovation and design in a thoughtful way.
    
-   **Inari Palmquist** — Exploring interior design through bespoke wallpapers and fabrics — a graceful way to bring artistry into living spaces, adding aesthetic and emotional value to everyday environments.
    
-   **Tiina Dia / Noumen Notes** — A creative endeavour that promises unique experiences. Though details are still emerging, the name alone evokes curiosity and invites people to follow the journey.
    
-   Sopu Oksanen **/ Tuntu** — An explorative concept combining art, music, movement and play — aiming to create immersive installations and experiences that resonate with audiences physically and emotionally.
    
-   Leonardo Rojnic **/** [VIR2OSO.com](http://VIR2OSO.com) [| Book Classical](https://vir2oso.com/) [Musicians in Finland](https://vir2oso.com)**—** booking platform for classical musicians in Finland
    
-   **Jin Oh &** [Gua Khee Chong](https://www.linkedin.com/in/guakhee?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAAD8xHYcBMVCEhjmaDfVmWJMMg5O8IgEBA5k) **/** **Meni Meri** — Tea houses downtown Helsinki infused with classical Chinese and Japanese musical performances and visual art — a cultural and sensory journey, offering rest, reflection and cross-cultural connection in an immersive space.
    
-   Yvapurü Jorge Samaniego Bonnin **&** Lorena Articardi **/ Yvapurü Studios Ry**— A collaborative platform connecting Latin American and Finnish artists — a bold ambition to nurture cross-cultural dialogue and creative exchange that transcends borders.
    

Words of support were also expressed to the Uniarts Hub project curators Riitta Huttunen and Jyri Partanen, who are instrumental in shaping a supportive, forward-thinking environment for emerging creative entrepreneurs.

In their closing remarks, the jury expressed encouragement to all pitching session participants to continue to be active and believe in themselves and their ideas. The event was successful and inspiring, and all projects are worthy of development and further support as their startups mature.
