Open House at Aalto Creatives Pre-incubator programme

Created on 2026-01-14

Published on 2026-01-14

Initiatives such as the Aalto Creatives Pre-incubator programme at Aalto University are truly inspiring. Open not only to students but to all curious and creative minds, the programme supports early-stage ideas through collaboration, experimentation, and innovative thinking — exactly what Finland needs right now.

The atmosphere of the event was perfectly captured by Aalto Creatives alumna Janina Weckström, who noted that in the world increasingly marked by constant negative happening and division, collaboration and creating something new and meaningful for the common good are more important than ever. Her words resonated strongly with the audience, as reflected in the warm and enthusiastic applause.

Several promising startups emerging from Aalto Creatives were presented, with founders openly sharing their progress and challenges. We have already planned follow-up interviews with some of the teams to better understand their current needs and potential collaboration opportunities they are seeking for.

It was also a pleasure to meet my mentee Shakib Emon, a Master's student at LUT University, whose research focuses on the application of AI in B2B sales and communications — a topic closely aligned with the collaborative and forward-looking spirit of the event.

"Yesterday I had the chance to visit the Aalto Creatives programme and honestly it was super inspiring. It was great to see how creative ideas slowly turn into actual businesses not just by having a good idea but by clearly understanding who it's for, what problem it solves and getting feedback early on. Also, watching founders work on their pitches was a good reminder that pitching is something you learn by doing and re-doing. Big thanks to my mentor Olga K. for the support and encouragement. It honestly means a lot. If you are playing with a creative idea and thinking about what's next, Aalto Creatives seems like a great place to explore it."

— Shakib Emon
