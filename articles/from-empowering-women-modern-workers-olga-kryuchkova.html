Created on 2023-08-30 15:16

After the traditional July holidays in Finland, work and project life intensify. One of the events worth mentioning was MentoraSTEAM Project (Vaasa) which I participated due to the great initiative of Liisa Kulta(Nicehearts) and Valentine Mukundwa(Nicehearts).

Participation in the MentoraSTEAM Project close-up meeting yielded profound benefits by fostering a dynamic exchange of innovative ideas and collaborative insights on boosting migrant women's employability in the STEAM sector. The meeting facilitated a comprehensive review of project milestones, allowing for real-time problem-solving and strategic adjustments. Through active engagement with diverse stakeholders, including mentors, mentees, experts from Vaasa University and TE-office, the meeting enriched participant perspectives and catalyzed cross-pollination of knowledge cooperation between diverse organizations in the region. By deepening relationships, enhancing project coherence, and refining employability strategies, this event ultimately propelled the MentoraSTEAM Project towards heightened efficiency and effectiveness in its mission to empower aspiring international women in science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics.

Organisers systematised employment challenges faced by highly-skilled women in Finland and offered practical strategies during masterclass to overcome these challenges:

  • Discrimination towards migrants and lack of trust,

  • Lack of relevant and necessary information,

  • Lack of stakeholders' involvement and commitment,

  • Difference between home country degree and host country requirements,

  • Lack of Finnish language skills and immigrant background.

This resonated with the recent public activity of Lucia Vuillermin and other migrant activists in the context of the proposed immigration reform and goes along with the values of International Working Women of Finland and International Professionals Finland organizations. This and some other organizations regularly offer beneficial offline meetings and webinars that can respond to the challenges mentioned above. For example, #1 Degree Recognition in Finland webinar is planned for September 12 and is open for registration already.
