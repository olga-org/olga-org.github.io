# [The Spirit of Piscine Lives On!](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/spirit-piscine-lives-olga-kryuchkova-bqqlf)

*Published on 2025-05-30 06:01*

Last Friday, May 23, something extraordinary happened with us— something that reminded us one more time why we chose the disruptive, ever changing, exhilarating world of IT in the first place. Hospitable [Hive Helsinki](https://www.linkedin.com/company/hive-helsinki/) is known as much for its rigorous bootcamps as for its caffeine-fueled camaraderie, in collaboration with [Print Room](https://www.linkedin.com/company/printroomhki/) (with workaholics in the person of my colleagues [Maria Loginova](https://www.linkedin.com/in/marialogi?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAADyNujUBd2m4IfVCAJhwWbjnvu5P_7t__cE) and [Laura Issakova](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lauissak?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAACBWX9UBI9y-sFJ3zkQFXV3A_R8vA1c2weo)), hosted a slogan competition that was nothing short of a celebration of wit, teamwork, and the supremacy of unbreakable Piscine spirit.

![Photo by Hive, Helsinki](/assets/Publications/hive1.jpg)

Yes, there were brackets. Yes, there were bugs (mostly in our speeches, because we are not professional orators 😀). But more than anything, there were brilliance of joy, inspiration and the “aha” moments.

![Photo by Hive, Helsinki](/assets/Publications/hive2.jpg)

Hive participants brought their A-game to the competition, showcasing not only their sharp programming minds but also their ability to inject humor into even the driest syntax. Choosing the winners? Let's just say it was harder than explaining recursion to a first-year CS student. Every slogan delivered a punchline that hit right in the feels — and occasionally right in the stack trace.

![Photo by Hive, Helsinki](/assets/Publications/hive3.jpg)

The event wasn’t just about slogans. It was a tribute to the culture Hive fosters — one of relentless curiosity, contagious energy, and collaborative growth. At Print Room, we see ourselves as living proof of that environment. We’ve not only become a solid team but also a little community that thrives on shared values, constant learning, and the occasional meme fights (because nothing says bonding like arguing whether tabs or spaces are superior, digital or printed product are better and so on 😀).

We want to express our heartfelt thanks to [Hive Helsinki](https://www.linkedin.com/company/hive-helsinki/) for being more than just a place to code for us. It’s a space where ideas bloom, friendships form, and teams like ours come to life. A special shout-out goes to [**Sofia**](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sofia-nordb%C3%A4ck-4b2613231/) and [Salla](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sallakettunen/) , who are the human equivalent of the unstoppable and essential when things get complicated. Their dedication to creating a welcoming, productive atmosphere has left a deep impact on all of us. It was easy to work with them.

![Photo by Hive, Helsinki](/assets/Publications/hive5.jpg)

To all the Piscine participants and Hive students — keep going! You’ve already shown what you're capable of when given a problem and a bit of caffeine. Keep collaborating! Keep building! Keep challenging the status quo (but maybe not in production).
