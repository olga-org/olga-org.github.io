### II. The Idea & Innovation

Olga: What’s the “aha” insight behind Mure AI?

Ahmed: Many enterprise AI products are either too complex, too expensive, or optimized for large corporations. Yet in Finland and across Europe, the backbone of the economy is small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). These organizations often lack the resources to build automation internally. Mure AI addresses this gap with a low-code platform that enables SMEs to automate routine tasks affordably—freeing employees to focus on creative and strategic work, rather than replacing them.

![Photo by Olga K.](/assets/Publications/mure6.jpg)

Olga: How do you plan de-risking your product? Where are you now?

Ahmed: The current focus is on delivering an MVP within the next six months, followed by security validation, pilot testing with early adopters, and the first official release. Essentially we wanted to make existing businesses more efficient and save them time through our agentic AI products but our final goal is to have a platform with these ready made solutions that people can just plug and play, and deploy instantly with little to no effort, integrated with their calendar, email or CRM system.

### III. Relevance to the Finnish, Nordic, and European Market

Olga: Which local or regional pain points do you plan to address?

Ahmed: In Finland and the Nordics, SMEs often face high labor costs and skill shortages, but cannot afford large-scale IT teams. For example, a small logistics firm may need customer support automation but cannot dedicate €100,000+ to develop it in-house. Mure AI offers a cost-effective, compliant alternative on demand and only when it is needed, that allows to lower pricing for the clients.

Olga: How do you adapt to multilingual, multi-jurisdiction environments?

Ahmed: From the start, compliance with GDPR and labor law is a design priority. The platform supports multilingual workflows and allows data localization when required by regulation. Architecture choices are guided by the need for data sovereignty and flexibility across jurisdictions.

![Photo by VTC](/assets/Publications/mure11.jpg)

Olga: How do you build trust in Nordic markets?

Ahmed: Trust comes from transparency. Nordic customers demand fairness, sustainability, and accountability. Mure AI adopts a transparent pricing model, clear documentation of data usage, and independent security audits. The main challenge is slower adoption cycles, but this is offset by long-term customer loyalty once trust is earned.

### IV. Tech, AI, Data & Ethics

Olga: What safeguards are in place for enterprise-grade AI?

Ahmed: Mure AI applies guardrails including bias testing, explainability checks, adversarial robustness, and adherence to ISO/IEC security standards. The goal is to make AI decisions auditable and predictable, not “black boxes.”

Olga: How do you balance data performance with privacy and compliance?

Ahmed: Data is processed under privacy-by-design principles: client data remains under their control, anonymization is applied where possible, and opt-in consent governs enrichment. For example, logs used to improve models are separated from personally identifiable information (PII).

![Photo by Mure AI](/assets/Publications/mure13.jpg)

Olga: How do you handle errors in human-AI interaction?

Ahmed: Voice and language modules degrade gracefully—uncertain responses trigger fallback clarifications rather than risky assumptions. Special attention is paid to dialects, non-native speakers, and edge cases to preserve user trust.

Olga: How do you ensure security at scale?

Ahmed: The platform integrates role-based access control, API rate limiting, incident monitoring, and periodic penetration tests. As a startup, we recognize reputational risk is existential, so security practices are embedded from the start rather than postponed.

Olga: What ethical values guide Mure AI?

Ahmed: Core values include transparency, fairness, user autonomy, and minimal data use. One example: despite pressure to accelerate development by harvesting third-party datasets, the team chose a slower but legally and ethically safer path by building only on compliant data sources.

### V. Vision & Future

Olga: What does success look like in five years?

Ahmed: Success is measured not only in revenue but in impact: thousands of SMEs across Europe using Mure AI to reclaim time, reduce costs, and empower employees rather than replace them. Internally, success means a team culture that scales without losing agility.

Olga: What is the most ambitious scenario?

Ahmed: If growth aligns with expectations, Mure AI could evolve into a pan-European automation platform, integrating sector-specific AI agents, and possibly expanding into adjacent verticals like public sector automation or sustainability reporting.

Olga: What is the biggest existential risk?

Ahmed: The most significant risks are regulatory changes (e.g. under the upcoming EU AI Act) and the rapid commoditization of AI models. Mitigation comes from focusing on compliance-first design and building value in workflow orchestration, where differentiation is stronger than in raw models.

![Photo by Mure AI](/assets/Publications/mure15.jpg)

Olga: How will you maintain culture and agility as you scale?

Ahmed: Practices being implemented early include distributed decision-making, transparent communication rituals, and a “documentation-first” culture to prevent bottlenecks as the team grows.

Olga: Which future trends could alter your strategy?

Ahmed: The rise of large multimodal models, decentralized infrastructures, and stricter AI regulations are the most likely disruptors. Mure AI is preparing by maintaining flexible architecture and monitoring regulatory developments closely.
