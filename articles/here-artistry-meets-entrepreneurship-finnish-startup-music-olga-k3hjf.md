# [Here Artistry Meets Entrepreneurship: The Finnish Startup Making Classical Music Sustainable](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/here-artistry-meets-entrepreneurship-finnish-startup-music-olga-k3hjf)

## I. Origins & Background

Olga K.: **How did your identity as a musician shape your decision to build** [VIR2OSO](https://VIR2OSO.com)**?**

Leonardo Rojnic: My journey as a classical musician taught me that exceptional musicianship alone isn’t enough. You need to be found by the right audience at the right moment. At the Sibelius Academy, I was surrounded by world-class musicians who had trained for decades, yet struggled to make a sustainable living with their art. We spent years perfecting our craft, but the path to meaningful bookings and opportunities is opaque and often transactional.

VIR2OSO emerged from that tension. As a performer, I understand the vulnerability of standing on stage, the years of preparation behind every note, the financial struggle that haunts even the best musicians. That’s why I am building VIR2OSO - the platform I wish had existed to help me navigate those same challenges.

I moved from Croatia to Finland at 18 to study at the Sibelius Academy since Finland is one of the global centers for classical accordion. What struck me most wasn’t just the musical excellence, but how Finnish institutions create space for artists to thrive. There’s a belief here that good systems should support creativity, not suffocate it. That’s VIR2OSO's foundation - I am building this from the musician’s perspective because I’ve lived it my entire life.

## II. The Idea & Innovation

**Olga: What unique problem does VIR2OSO solve?**

**Leonardo:** When I started exploring this space, I thought the problem was simply access - making it easier to book musicians. But I quickly learned the real problem is trust. Most existing platforms prioritize scale over quality - open directories where anyone can list themselves, with inconsistent pricing and no vetting process. Clients are often left guessing whether they’re booking excellence or taking a gamble.

VIR2OSO solves the trust problem through curation and transparency. Our artists are top musicians from institutions like the Finnish National Opera, Finnish Radio-Symphony Orchestra, Sibelius Academy and Helsinki Philharmonic Orchestra. Clients know they’re booking excellence, and not risking on an unknown. Musicians know they’re joining a curated community where they compete on artistry, not price. It’s a harder model to scale than an open marketplace, but it’s the only sustainable moat.

**Olga: How do you balance platform logic with the “emotional” side of music?**

**Leonardo:** I believe that technology should amplify humanity, not replace it. VIR2OSO’s platform handles the transactional friction which includes transparent pricing, streamlined booking, secure payment, guaranteed replacements if cancellations happen - so the human connection can flourish.  

Every artist profile includes video performances, personal stories, and repertoire specialties - not just star ratings and price points. What we’re building toward is deeper storytelling and connection before the booking itself - helping clients make the best choice for their specific moment.

### III. European Innovation & Cultural Ecosystem

**Olga: How does VIR2OSO combine entrepreneurship and artistry?**

**Leonardo:** I want VIR2OSO to be the proof that these worlds aren’t opposed, but rather complementary. **Entrepreneurship without artistry can be soulless.** Artistry without entrepreneurship is often invisible. I’ve lived both sides: the honor of performing as a soloist with the big symphony orchestra, and the frustration of struggling to pay the bills despite the constant pursuit of excellence and years of training.

I want VIR2OSO’s model to show that you can build a profitable, scalable business while elevating culture. That’s the future of Europe’s creative economy: artists who understand business, entrepreneurs who value art, and platforms that serve both. I see massive potential in this model - not just for music, but for visual arts, theatre, dance, and beyond. This is just the beginning.

**Olga: How does VIR2OSO contribute to preserving and democratizing classical music?**

**Leonardo:** By removing the gatekeepers without lowering the bar. Classical music has long been seen as elitist, expensive, and hard to access. VIR2OSO aims to change that with transparent pricing, vetted artists you can book in minutes, live classical music anywhere - not just formal concert halls.  

We’re also creating sustainable income streams for musicians, which is essential for the art form’s survival. Many musicians who trained for decades struggle for their survival nowadays. If artists can’t make a living, classical music dies. VIR2OSO ensures they thrive while introducing new audiences to the art of live classical music.

### IV. Tech, Ethics & Experience

**Olga: Do you worry about commoditization of classical music because of platforms like VIR2OSO?**

**Leonardo:** I do. Platforms can reduce art to transactions if designed carelessly. **But I also believe thoughtful platforms can elevate art by creating context, storytelling, and connection that wouldn’t exist otherwise**. VIR2OSO emphasizes the experience, not just the service. If we do our job right, clients book on VIR2OSO not just because it’s convenient, but because it offers something deeper - an experience, and a memory they’ll carry for years to come.

I’ve heard “horror stories” about platforms with massive margins where artists didn’t even know the final price clients paid. That’s not us. We take 15% commission from musicians - only after they’ve been paid for a successful performance. We want to enable performances, not limit them - but we also won’t sacrifice quality for growth. I believe that this curation over volume, experience over transaction, and musicians competing on artistry, not price - is what separates us from commoditization.

### V. Vision & Future

**Olga: Where do you see VIR2OSO in 5-10 years?**

**Leonardo:** In 5 years, I see VIR2OSO as a go-to platform for premium live classical music across Finland and expanding into the Nordics. In 10 years, I see us as a movement - not just a booking platform, but an ecosystem that proves artists can thrive without compromising their art. We’ll expand geographically and potentially into other art forms, but always with the same principles: curation, transparency, and artist sustainability. The mission scales, not just the platform. Ultimately, VIR2OSO should become what I wished existed when I was starting out - a place where exceptional artists are discovered, valued, and sustained.

**Olga: What keeps you personally motivated beyond profitability?**

**Leonardo: My mission is creating moments that inspire both artists and audiences**. Every booking on VIR2OSO represents a musician earning sustainable income and a client experiencing something special. That dual impact is what drives me through the tough days. I’m funding this out of my own pocket, learning entrepreneurship from scratch, and taking risks that feel uncomfortable but necessary. I believe musicians deserve better, and clients deserve confidence. Seeing that happen is worth every risk.

I haven’t abandoned music; I’m still performing, but now I’m also building something that helps other artists as well. My mission remains the same: creating memorable musical experiences that inspire both artists and audiences, just on different levels.  To capture it in one sentence - VIR2OSO is about curated quality, the belief that accessing world-class music shouldn’t be complicated, and that life as an artist should be sustainable. That’s the future we’re building.

**Olga: What type of support or collaboration are you looking for?**

**Leonardo:** I’m looking for three types of collaboration:

-   Artist Community: Professional classical musicians ready to join a curated platform that prioritizes artistry and creates sustainable income opportunities.
    
-   Insights and Mentorship: Entrepreneurs who've built trust-based marketplaces or scaled creative platforms. I’m learning as I build and value honest feedback.
    

If any of this resonates, or you know someone who should be involved, I’d love to connect.
