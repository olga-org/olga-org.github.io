# [Inside the Startup Dream: How Nordic Beyond Entertainment Is Building Finland’s Bridge to Global Audiences](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/inside-startup-dream-how-nordic-beyond-entertainment-olga-kryuchkova-hqaxf)

*Published on 16-10-2025, 11 min read*

Today we’re speaking with Fangda Chen, and Arpit Jain the co-founders of Nordic Beyond Entertainment Oy **(NBE)**—a Finnish startup that blends creativity, culture, and digital storytelling. NBE is already partnering with projects like SPLASH! **Forum**, where they bring their energy to social media promotion, and TUNTU **– Sense the Experience**, which designs nature-inspired immersive spaces for businesses.

At the same time, Fangda and his team are exploring new collaborations, including potential social media partnerships with international entrepreneurs, while also building toward a bold vision: hosting **Best Ever Food Review Show’s** **(BEFRS)** visit in Finland and showcasing Finnish food and travel to global audiences.

With roots in the Finnish startup ecosystem and ambitions to connect local creativity with international visibility, NBE stands at the intersection of **media, culture, and innovation**.

### I. Origins & CEO’s Background

Olga K. **: Please tell us about each member of your team and a bit about your backgrounds. What are your slogans?**

**Fangda (Founder & CEO):** Hi, I’m a Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences, hospitality management alumni—graduated in December 2024—with three years of front‑office experience in hotels. I started in January 2022 as a trainee (for two months), moved to receptionist (in nine months), and then handled front office shift leader duties alongside my receptionist role for the last two years during my career in the hotel industry.

I ended my hotel career in December 2024, one month before graduation. After graduating, I began my entrepreneurship journey as a **light entrepreneur (in Finnish toiminimi)** for the first half of 2025 (Jan–May). In June 2025, after the spark to build NBE’s bold vision, I started working full‑time as **Founder & CEO of NBE**—and I’ve been pushing our ambitious projects ever since.

**Arpit (Co‑founder & CFO):** Hi, I’m Arpit. I’m a third‑year finance student at Haaga‑Helia—yes, I’m still studying while building a company.

I come from an entrepreneurial background and have always been curious about how businesses grow. I’ve worked in finance for my dad and spent about six months as a logistics consultant. **Olga: How was the collaboration—and Nordic Beyond Entertainment—born?**

Fangda: We look at the big picture and believe *people are the ones* creating everything in the startup world. The idea of collaborations is tied to our journey to attract **Best Ever Food Review Show** (YouTube’s food & travel creator) and to make sure the video series about Finland’s food, travel, and culture is truly well done. For that, we need Finnish local businesses as collaborators, organizations that care about promoting Finnish goods and culture, and many more partners who can help us bring BEFRS to Finland and create a great, memorable story for the world to see.

The idea for **BEFRS in Finland** came extremely spontaneously. Me (Fangda) was training at the gym when, out of nowhere, BEFRS popped into my mind. I love their content—I’ve followed them since middle school (about eight years)—and my passion has always been food, travel, and social media content creation. So I came up with the conclusion: “Why not invite BEFRS to Finland?”. We host their visit and support their content productions throughout their trip in Finland. That’s how it started: a spark of an idea—and then we hit the “**All gas, no brakes”.**

### III. The Finnish Startup & Innovation Ecosystem

**Olga: Finland is famous for gaming, education, and design—where does NBE fit, and where will you challenge norms?**

Fangda: We’ll be known for the **entertainment** side—bringing joy to people who follow our journey and collaborate with us. We’re ready to do things unconventionally, maybe even controversially, and we’re unapologetic about it. We want to stand out and make creative works people haven’t seen before.

**Olga: What’s one thing international founders misunderstand about starting up in Finland?**

Fangda: Finland is small, and there are only a few major players in each field. People often know each other within the same industry. Most likely people you encounter in Finland will stick around longer than what you anticipate.

You should invest in your **brand and reputation** (personal and business) so the market perceives you the way you want.

**Olga: How does *sisu* (perseverance, resilience) show up in your projects or culture?**

Fangda: *Sisu* for us means discipline of **not** pleasing people by doing what already exists. We create boldly—things consumers didn’t know they wanted. We experiment continuously and have the courage to make work people might debate. Our goal is to set future standards for social‑media content creation and become an industry leader in the field.

**Olga: If Finland’s startup ecosystem were a music festival, what stage would NBE be on right now?**

Fangda: **Absolutely the** Slush **stage**—the main event (And yes, shaking hands with Alexander Stubb is on my bucket list) 😎

**Olga: How do collaborations with institutions like** Visit Finland **or Haaga‑Helia shape your projects—and Finland’s international cultural identity?**

Fangda: It’s significant. Haaga‑Helia is where I (Fangda) studied hospitality / hotel management and got my first touch of working life. NBE wouldn’t exist without Haaga‑Helia. Arpit is also a Haaga‑Helia BBA student. Many leaders in Finnish sports, business, marketing, and hospitality come from Haaga‑Helia—so Haaga-Helia does influence the direction of Finland’s economy and society. I’m proud to be an alumni.

Visit Finland is the one organization laser‑focused on promoting Finnish culture and travel. **What makes NBE unique:** Finnish & Nordic identity + food & travel + entertainment—spreading the lesser‑known parts of the Nordics to international audiences through our social media.

### IV. Tech, AI, Data & Ethics

**Olga: How do you use AI without losing the “human spark” of creativity?**

Fangda: I use AI for things I’m not specialized at or that are heavy lifting: business admin, structuring ideas, drafting captions (e.g., YouTube), and making sure writing is easy to read—often finalizing with ChatGPT for clarity. Where I avoid AI is **video editing itself** (I’ll ask for tips, but the edit is mine). Our content features **real people at live events**, not AI‑generated video. Authenticity is part of NBE’s identity. AI helps us scale the boring parts so we can focus on the human parts. That said, AI is everywhere—from photo correction to auto‑captions. We use tools, but the story stays human.

**Olga: If AI could simulate Finnish nature perfectly in VR, would that diminish the real‑life experience of Finland?**

Fangda: I don’t believe VR—or future tech—can perfectly simulate Finnish nature. But if it *did*, then sure: save your plane ticket and experience it where the simulation is available. So yes, a perfect simulation would diminish the real thing. Still, I don’t believe tech will capture the full feeling. For that, you need the **real deal in Finland**. And I’m a believer in “Everything is impossible until someone does it”—so prove me wrong.

**Olga: How do you approach data ethics in social media and promotional projects for global audiences?**

Fangda: Great question. We’re at the very beginning, focused on getting on our feet. For our own NBE channels and my personal brand **@1andonlyconnoisseur**, I follow common‑sense and best practices learned over 15+ years on social media. As we scale into a stable **scale‑up**, we’ll formalize data‑ethics guidelines and training so everyone follows through and we set a good example.

**Olga: Have you encountered a moment where technology felt like “too much”—and how did you step back?**

Fangda: **NEVER.** Technology for a media startup is like money—**never too much** when it serves the mission.

**Olga: What role should creative companies like NBE play in setting ethical standards for AI in entertainment and culture?**

Fangda: Lead by example. Encourage practices that **don’t** generate content violating ethical standards or harming audiences. Keep credit and consent clear.

### V. Vision & Future of Nordic Beyond Entertainment Oy

**Olga: What would NBE look like in five years if everything goes according to your most ambitious vision?**

Fangda: Honestly, I haven’t planned that far. I’ve planned the next two years—and it’s going to be a roller coaster if all goes well. There’s a **Malaysian Unc 🇲🇾** I’d love to see give Finland a try after BEFRS 😉🇫🇮

In five years, I can imagine being on a **PJ (private jet)** with the team, flying around the world to make even more remarkable things happen. But I’m young with a dream, so I’m focused on what moves the needle **now**.

**Olga: What partnerships or support would help NBE most right now?**

Fangda: Institutions and people only—because that’s what’s in my mind right now: **Alexander Stubb, Visit Finland, Haaga‑Helia,** Hungry for Finland**, BEFRS**, just to name a few. If you’re aligned with our bold, ambitious, and unprecedented vision, **our hands are open**.

**Olga: Which do you dream of more: NBE as a household name in Finland, or a bridge for Finland to the global stage?**

Fangda: From day one: **be the bridge**. We’re not here just to make a living or stroke our egos. We’re here to make a statement, show what’s possible, and set trends that make the world a better place to live.

**Olga: Imagine your future company culture—what ritual would reflect your mission?**

Fangda: For who I am and who I want NBE to be—**“win‑win‑win, never average.”** I’ll always encourage big swings and accept that mistakes happen. Just don’t repeat the same mistake, and keep improving.

The culture we avoid: following norms, doing average, mid, boring work. That’s not us. We keep the pioneer, norm‑breaking, trend‑setting spirit alive.

If someone is consistently mid and boring, I’ll **replace them with spicier individuals**. That’s NBE.

As a founder, I also want to make sure that when we hit success—money flowing in and it’s hard to spend it all—we stay **humble** and spend wisely (maybe funding companies that make a positive impact).

**Olga: If you could write a headline about NBE in 2029’s international press, what would it be?**

Fangda: *The Headline: “From a gym’s pec‑fly machine to a* ***$50M*** *private jet.”*

Basically, a headline that speed‑runs our beginning and how we broke through—so I ended up on that $50M PJ.
