# [The Startup Whisperer: How a Lifelong Learner Turned Nordic Curiosity into a Venture Philosophy](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/startup-whisperer-how-lifelong-learner-turned-nordic-olga-kryuchkova-yyt7f)

*Published on 03-10-2025, 15 min read*

On a sunlit afternoon in Söderkulla, we sat down with [Risto Rautakorpi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ristorautakorpi?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAAAKwQaUBONewGq_nOrJxAGgYS6-QvD0tdK4) to reflect on both the evolution of [**Gorilla Capital**](https://gorillacapital.fi/) and the arc of his own remarkable journey. From launching his first company at 22, to shaping strategy at Microsoft during its high-growth years, and eventually returning to the grassroots world of start-ups, Risto has become one of the most trusted voices in Nordic venture capital.

Often described as a *“start-up whisperer”* — a guide who helps founders discern the unspoken truths of building businesses — Risto now serves as **Founding and General Partner at Gorilla Capital**, a fund known as the Nordics’ “institutional super angel.” Across Gorilla’s three funds, Risto and his team have invested in about 150 companies, combining capital with what founders often call “*brutal honesty, practical wisdom, and relentless focus on fundamentals*“.

In this conversation, we explore Gorilla Capital’s role within Finland’s business, tech and innovation landscape, while also tracing the path that shaped Risto himself — a journey from curious entrepreneur to corporate leader, and ultimately to a venture builder who redefines what sustainable growth means for start-ups.

### I. Origins & Founder’s Background

Olga K. : With 40+ years in IT and a role in Microsoft’s Xbox launch, which moment from that era still influences how you mentor early-stage founders today?

Risto: I don't think it's any particular moment as such. It's rather the cumulative experience and all the intuition built based on all those experiences. My Microsoft years were a very important part of that. It was no coincidence Microsoft became such a success story. Every day I refer to something I learned during my 13 years there.

**Olga K.:** You hold Master’s degree in Engineering from LUT University and MBA is from [Helsingin kauppakorkeakoulu](https://www.linkedin.com/company/uifmb/) , which is today part of [Aalto University](https://www.linkedin.com/company/aalto-university/). How do those dual lenses—technical vs. commercial—show up when you're assessing a startup pitch?

**Risto:** The engineer in me definitely wants to understand how things work - to identify cause-and-effect patterns and to look under the hood. So I do with start-ups the same thing I would do as an engineer for any new device. I break it into pieces to see how it works and what it's made of. But the engineering aspect is not the key. **I'm always more keen on the benefit the product or service can produce** than the widget itself, so the commercial aspect is ultimately the part that matters. **As investors, we focus on business, not product or tech**.

**Olga K.:** From “startup whisperer” to “camel investor,” what personal traits do you think have guided your journey most—curiosity, resilience, or something more mysterious?

**Risto:** Nothing mysterious I believe, but curiosity and being a learner are key things. I really want to understand why things are the way they are, why people do what they do. What is the purpose? What's behind it? How does it work? And I ask as long as needed for me to understand and get it. Or for me to conclude that it's not understandable, at least not by me.

Another thing is that I have always seen change more as an opportunity than as a threat. That's the attitude startups have as well. So I guess I'm a good fit with the startup culture.

![Photo by Olga K.](/assets/Publications/ristor1.jpg)

**Olga K.:** Is there a childhood project or hobby (maybe tinkering with machines in a Nordic cabin) that planted the seed for your approach to venture building?

**Risto:** For me, the story of how I became a VC is the same as for most - it wasn't a plan, I just ended up here. But if I look back to my childhood and listen to what my parents had to tell, since a very early age, I've been a curious mind and a builder. I want to learn to understand, and I want to build something. I am keen on many, many things. Today I have more hobbies than I have time to spend on them. So I guess it’s some kind of an open mind attitude which is keen to learn.

**Olga K.:** Do you recall a failure or setback early in your career that still shapes how you validate founders’ mindset today?

**Risto:** Yes, but unfortunately it wasn't at such an early stage. I was around 40 when things started going sideways, and it hurts way more when it happens at that age vs had it happened like 20 years earlier.

I was complacent and I thought I knew, but the reality was that I didn't have a clue. **A start-up is a learning project, and to be able to learn, one must be humble**. So **we evaluate the founder's ability to learn and their humility** very carefully. A complacent and cocky attitude are instant deal killers.

### II. The Idea & Innovation

**Olga K.:** You coined the “camel” investing model—how did that imagery come alive for you? Was there a particular startup or story that sparked it?

**Risto:** Like many other things we actively promote, this wasn't something we originally invented ourselves. We first picked this concept up from a [Harvard Business Review article](https://hbr.org/2020/10/startups-its-time-to-think-like-camels-not-unicorns) a few years ago, and we've since adopted it because it seems to work really well - even people who hear it for the first time do get the essence of it

 **Olga K.:** Given your focus on what’s **likely to happen**, how do you balance that with recognizing wild, creative disruptions—are they overlooked?

**Risto:** Wild creative disruptions usually refer to the outliers, which are more of a double-or-nothing game than something somewhat predictable. Those double or nothing cases just are not a fit for us. But they are overlooked in a sense that they grab all media attention – which can be a bit dangerous as it gives a very biased perspective to an uninformed reader. It can set wrong expectations.

![Photo by Olga K.](/assets/Publications/ristor3.jpg)

 **Olga K.:** What’s a surprising misconception you’ve encountered about “data-driven” investing from founders or peers?

**Risto:** I don't know if this is surprising, but definitely a very typical misconception is to mix assumptions with data. I.e. treat assumptions as data, which is dangerous because only what has been proven to work in real life is data. Everything else is just hypotheses and they should be regarded as such.

**Olga K.:** If you were to invent a new animal metaphor to describe a radically different investing style (unrelated to unicorns or camels), what would it be and why?

**Risto:** I don't think I can invent a radically different investing style because there are already practitioners for all walks of life and all kinds of strategies. But now since you ask, maybe a **giraffe investor -  investing in founders who have their heads in the clouds all the time.**

**Olga K.:** In what ways has [GorillaCast](https://gorillacapital.fi/insights/) influenced your own thinking about innovation—any episode or guest that shifted your perspective?

**Risto:** Sorry to kill your excitement: actually, I am the one who has been selecting the guests for the GorillaCast shows based on me knowing that they have an interesting story to tell. So as such I have not learned anything new because I knew their stories already beforehand.

### III. Relevance of Gorilla Capital to Finnish Tech & Innovation Ecosystem

**Olga K.:** How has Gorilla Capital's “camel investor” ethos reshaped expectations for early-stage Finnish founders—not just in funding but mindset?

**Risto:** I hope massively! **We have a big mission to educate the whole startup ecosystem about our alternative**. A famous chef has said “how do you know which food you like until you have tasted everything”. S**tartup founders should know of all alternatives so that they can make an informed decision about the strategy** that they feel most comfortable with.

![Photo by Olga K.](/assets/Publications/ristor6.jpg)

**Olga K.:** With over 150 investments reviewed and made, what ecosystem pattern (e.g. regional, technical, cultural) uniquely stands out to you in Finland?

**Risto:** The fact that **most founders are first timers** – **that’s just a result of the still relatively short history of startups in Finland**. **A serial founder has a big advantage over first timers, but there are no shortcuts in getting there, we simply need more founders to go the full journey and be willing to start again. And share their learnings with others!**

**Olga K.:** What’s an overlooked strength of Finnish founders that you wish more international investors would appreciate?

**Risto:** Finns are honest, modest and hard workers. The cost of knowledge workers is very competitive in Finland, especially vs US. Everyone speaks decent English.

**Olga K.:** Can you share a moment when Gorilla Capital’s entry into a startup fundamentally changed the game—maybe unlocking partnerships, credibility, or catalyzing ecosystem shifts?

**Risto: “I wish someone had told me this years ago” is a comment we here too often.** Too often because by not knowing about this alternative (to the mainstream stereotypes) way to grow a startup a founder was forced to “pretend” to be and do something she wasn’t comfortable with and wasted years in pursuing the wrong goals. What she heard from us lit the bulb in her head and that caused a seismic change.

### IV. Approaches to Choose Startups to Support, Methodology

**Olga K.:** You talk about “assessing thousands of startups” and seeing patterns—what’s one unusual signal that nine times out of ten correlates with later success?

**Risto:** It’s never just one thing but if **I can only name one: a humble attitude coupled with a passion to understand customer problem and draw insights from it**. This is the key fundamental, that alone does not guarantee success but it is impossible to succeed without it.

**Olga K.:** Your founders say your sparring includes analogies like “moose hunting” or “poker chips.” What’s your favorite metaphor to convey a subtle but critical startup truth?

**Risto:** There are many and I create new ones if needed but one I seem to have used a lot recently is the concept of a boat’s hull speed: there is a “**hull speed**” for the speed at which a company can grow. Exceeding that results in a big waste.

**Olga K.:** How do you personally test for founder-sustainability—in terms of mental stamina, product grit, or weathering months of ambiguity?

**Risto:** Predicting future is hard at best. **The best proxy is to look at their past.** What have they done & achieved so far, how have they behaved in different situations, what drives their behaviour. **What is their “Why**”, that is very important. How realistic expectations do they have. I try to assess whether that person can pull it off.

**Olga K.:** Is there a “choice tool” or litmus test—maybe in mind or Excel—that helps you know when to walk away quickly?

**Risto:** We are not the right investor for over 90% of the cases offered to us so our eyes are very trained to spot the indicators suggesting this is not for us. We try to spot them early for not to waste anyone’s time. The attitude (humble vs complacent) is a root cause for many visible parameters – round size, valuation, expectations on future etc.

**Olga K.:** In balancing diversification vs. focused backing, when does a startup standout enough to deviate from your model’s broad spread?

**Risto:** Never. We have to stick to our strategy, that’s what our investors have given us their money for. Winner picking is a different strategy with a different risk profile – we leave that for others.

### V. Tech, AI, Data & Ethics

**Olga K.:** As “camel investing” leans on probability, in your view how should early-stage founders responsibly build AI-driven ventures without overhyping?

**Risto: AI is just a tool. A very powerful one, but still just a tool.** “AI inside” doesn’t alone make a case better. But it is a big productivity driver and founders must learn to take full advantage of it. Their competitors certainly will, so if they don’t they’ll lose.

![Photo by Olga K.](/assets/Publications/ristor9.jpg)

**Olga K.:** Have you encountered ethical dilemmas—for instance, AI startups with data privacy concerns—and how do you approach them in diligence?

**Risto:** Not much, and nothing AI specific yet. Our Investment Policy – part of our agreement with our investors – contains also some guidelines in such matters. There have been some grey area cases which we had to consider very carefully, but eventually the decisions were very clear.

**Olga K.:** Do you see opportunities where AI could enhance your investment decision-making—pattern recognition, founder screening, outcome modeling?

**Risto:** Ah, this is the area for modern era alchemists! I quote Mike Maples, Jr (an experienced VC) who in his recent podcast explained to his guest how he has built several AI models to evaluate the prospects based on various strategies and winning formulas. “Does it work?” his guest asked. “Rounding it to the nearest Yes or No, the answer is No” said Mike. The AI tools may help in the analysis, but the ultimate question whether to invest or not still needs a human to answer. And I expect this to be the case for some time to come.

**Olga K.:** With data-driven insights central to your model, how do you guard against bias—even subtle ones—in evaluating startups from diverse backgrounds?

**Risto:** We believe 100% in meritocracy. We look at the data, results, facts. We are blind and immune to everything else. We are completely un-emotional. (We once analysed the % of female founders getting funding from us. The result: exactly the same % as in the applications. As it should be, if there is no bias one way or the other)

**Olga K.:** If you could commission a startup to solve one systemic ethical challenge using AI (outside of venture capital), what would it be and why?

**Risto:** This is political rather than ethical, but I’d really like to hear the politically unbiased opinion of AI, based on only objective facts, of what would be the most feasible method to arrange healthcare in Finland. Why? Political decision making is not based on facts or logical reasoning and I believe that results in a massive waste of money and resources.

### VI. Vision & Future

**Olga K.:** Ten years from now, how will we describe a “camel company”—what altitude have camels soared to in the global startup narrative?

**Risto:** The ”camel strategy” is timeless as such – as its cornerstones are the business fundamentals: revenue and profit. Real business has been based on those since Day 1 and so will it be going forward. Zero interest rate era was an anomaly which created many unsustainable practices. But the narrative is always focused on what is seen as fashionable or sexy. Travelling like a camel is not a fashion and it is not sexy. Hence I don’t think it will ever dominate the headlines. Which is only a good thing!

**Olga K.:** What's an emerging tech frontier—quantum, biotech, deep sustainability—where you hope to see a “camel” model succeed?

**Risto:** There are businesses which are inherently capital intensive (you name a few!), there is no way to avoid having to expose a large amount of money up front. But one could be a “camel” within the peer group, demonstrating capital efficiency relative to others. That would certainly be a massive advantage and I challenge people within such vertical to think from the perspective of “how could this be done with least money possible”.

**Olga K.:** If you could send a message to your younger self at the start of Gorilla Capital, what would that be and why?

**Risto:** We started about 15 years ago so I was not young even then! But maybe this: Trust your own judgement! Some of the biggest mistakes as an investor we have made when we allowed  ourselves to be distracted by what some others (high profile investors) did. Everyone assesses the prospects based on their own criteria, so their conclusions do not necessarily apply to you. Make your own decisions based on your own assessment.

**Olga K.:** Beyond Exits Summit and GorillaCast, what formats or new community infrastructure do you think would be the next frontier for founder-investor dialogue?

**Risto:** Right now we are thinking a lot about ways to support our portfolio company founders. Primary objective continues to be to speed up the learning to reach PMF faster. With some new additions to the Gorilla team, we can now provide guidance also on Product Discovery. We offer “clinic hours” for founders to book sparring sessions. We help founders set up right objectives and key activities to increase focus and productivity. And so on, we keep experimenting to find the tricks that work best.

**Olga K.:** In your cabin reflections, do you imagine a future where founders lead with realism and clarity over hype—is that your enduring legacy?

**Risto:** The world needs also those who take irrational risks to reach breakthroughs. But that is not the right approach, nor the one founders feel comfortable with, for most. In the perfect world the new founder knows about the various alternative paths – and their inherent ups and downs - they could choose from, and makes an informed decision about the path they choose to follow.

**Olga K.:** My final question will be: what forms of partnership, collaboration, or external support would be most valuable in advancing GorillaCapital’s role and future initiatives within the emerging Finnish tech and innovation ecosystem?

**Risto:** Our goal is that no founder would ever need to say **“I wish someone had told me this years ago”** again. To reach that, we need more people than ourselves to spread the gospel. Regardless of whether that founder ends up working with us or not, she should know to be able to make informed choices. So if reading this article lit a bulb in your head dear reader, do a favour to your colleagues and help them experience an epiphany as well!
