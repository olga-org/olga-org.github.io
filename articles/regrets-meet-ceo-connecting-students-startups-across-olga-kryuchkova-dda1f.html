"No Regrets": Meet the CEO Connecting Students with Startups Across Europe

Created on 2025-07-29 14:28

Published on 2025-07-30 10:21

I. Origins & CEO’s Background

Olga K.:  Alexander, what personal drive led you from your early career into leading a student–startup matchmaking platform?

Alexander Warén: It started from when I ran for president in my bachelor’s program – it was the first time I had ever properly exited my comfort zone to grow. I joined Strive originally as Head of Operations to continue this learning journey and never expected to be in this position now, but when it was offered to me, I decided to keep going. No regrets with that decision!

Olga: Was there a moment working with universities that convinced you these connections were worth scaling nationally?

Alexander: I can’t pinpoint an exact moment but I think just seeing the excitement from other communities when they learned about what we do is definitely something that fuels my internal fire. Talking to people who have actually found jobs through our platform and event are the best parts of the job, and seeing that we can make an impact, even to just one person, shows me that there is potential in other places outside of Finland.

Olga: What unexpected lesson about community emerged when you organized your first Startup Crawl?

Alexander: I think the biggest lesson was understanding that nothing will ever go exactly according to the original plan, and you always need to pivot and make decisions on the fly to ensure things go smoothly. There were a bunch of things that came up last year that I never would have even expected, but going into it this year, I’m much more equipped to deal with any fire that might come up.

Olga: Which early-founder habit of yours still guides Strive’s daily operations?

Alexander: Not really sure if it’s a habit, but I feel like I try to lead as horizontally as possible, and I think it’s had a positive impact on the team. I always love hearing new ideas, because I know myself that I don’t have all the answers. Being able to see the strengths of each team member is something that I think really drives us forward.

Olga:   Who was the unsung mentor or advisor who reshaped your thinking about talent ecosystems?

Alexander: I don’t think I’ve had a particular mentor throughout the journey, but just getting to know others around the ecosystem and asking them for advice has been something that has definitely shaped my learning.

II. The Idea & Innovation

Olga:   Strive solves the multi-app fatigue for students—how did that user pain surface in your own student or early-career days?

Alexander: Great question. I was one of those people in the past who was struggling to find anything, so I understand the pain and the anxiety behind it. This culminated at my first startup, where I helped software developers upgrade their CVs and LinkedIn’s. The disconnection was very distinct, and I met so many people who had great skills but just didn’t know how to present themselves in the right way.

Olga:   Matching is algorithmic—but how do you preserve serendipity, bringing startup energy to unlikely yet powerful pairings?

Alexander: I think this really summarizes what the Startup Crawl is – of course we want to give students a choice in who they visit, but especially last year, so many connections were made at the event just by chance, and that’s something I definitely want to preserve in future startup crawls.

Olga:   What experiment in Startup Match or Crawl failed in a surprising or enlightening way?

Alexander: Funny story actually – we had our own food being cooked at the venue last year, and the original idea was to cook and serve the food on the inside, especially since it was raining. What ended up happening was people lining up outside, in the rain, just to get a bowl of pyttipannu and people didn’t mind! Definitely wasn’t the original plan but it ended up working just fine!

Olga:  How do you balance speed of placement with the depth of onboarding needed for student success?

Alexander: Usually this onboarding is done on the company’s side after a hire is completed, but we always share some information about the company first to the job seekers to ensure they actually want the job – consent is super important!

Olga:  Have you ever stopped a placement mid-way based on community feedback—even if metrics looked solid?

Alexander: No, but there are a few companies I’ve heard of that have a not-so-great culture, so I try to avoid those now.

III. Relevance of Finnish Job Market & Talents

Olga: Finland has ‘sisu’ and high trust—how do such cultural traits shine through in Strive matches?

Alexander: I think with this, we focus on transparency on both ends – making sure that the job seeker (student in this case) is actually interested in the job and the company, and ensuring that the company in question shares everything about the position (e.g. pay, working hours, benefits).

Olga: What Finnish educational or societal norms give your students an edge in fast-moving startup roles?

Alexander: I think it’s really all about the mindset – I think a lot of people in Finland don’t necessarily believe in the growth mindset of a startup and also don’t believe in their own abilities – I know because I was one of these people before. I think just having a passion to grow and try new things is the best attitude for a fast-paced startup!

Olga: Can people from the street, not student join startups using Strive?

Alexander: Yes, everyone is welcome! We of course focus on students and recent graduates, but everyone is allowed to join our events regardless of when they finished studying.

Olga: What skills—soft or technical—do Finnish students lack when matched with global-ready startups?

Alexander: I think this is a nuanced question but in general I think it’s the social skills and risk-averseness that many people suffer from.

Olga:  What could international ecosystems learn from Strive’s Finnish model of centralized student engagement?

Alexander: I think that the simplicity of at least the event is something that’s important – most job fairs I’ve been to have been almost useless because even the connections you make feel superficial and there’s no way to continue talking. At Strive, we just get everyone in the same room (students, founders, operators) along with having the platform ready, and we just let the magic happen!

IV. Tech, AI, Data & Ethics

Olga:  Does Strive use data to predict ‘start-up cultural fit’? If so, how do you guard against unconscious bias?

Alexander: We don’t do this yet, but we ensure that unconscious bias is minimized by doing multiple rounds of shortlisting.

Olga: In the matching tech, how do you reconcile data-driven matches with human intuition from mentors?

Alexander: We’re still in the process of building our new platform, so I guess this is coming soon!

Olga: How do you ensure interns’ privacy and agency in a platform-centric process?

Alexander: We ensure that everyone’s contact info (email, phone number) stays with us until we have the consent of the person to share that info with companies.

Olga:  As AI tools evolve, would you let startups deploy automated candidate scoring—or insist on human review?

Alexander: I think a human-AI balance is super important, especially in this time. Of course we’ll have AI processes going on to aid in the matchmaking, but we always want to ensure a human touch as well since sometimes AI can make mistakes.

Olga: Have you ever pulled back a tech feature due to ethical concerns raised from within the Strive community?

Alexander: Not yet, hopefully we won’t have to!

V. Vision & Future

Olga:  In five years, if Strive is a blueprint, what global ecosystem would look like—what role would your past self play?

Alexander: I would hope that in 5 years Strive is seen as a role-model for student-to-startup recruitment, not just in the Nordics but maybe even in Europe as a whole! I feel like I’d love to be a mentor to others who would be building solutions in the same field.

Olga: If the platform could advise every Finnish university, what systemic student-launch guide would you offer?

Alexander: At least from what I can think of right now, I think education on startups and entrepreneurship is a great starting point, especially since there’s many students who might not know the space who would love it!

Olga: At scale, how will you keep the startup–student bond personal?

Alexander: This is a great question – Hard to say right now since future pivots might happen, but I think that we’ll always have a soft spot for students, since currently its run by them.

Olga: What tension excites you more: automation of matching, or the chaos of unmediated student passion?

Alexander: I think the ladder – I’ve never been much of a tech person but love to see people’s passion.

Olga: Looking back, what legacy do you hope Strive leaves in the Finnish—and global—talent economy?

Alexander: I hope that Strive can become a staple of the ecosystem, similar to Slush – but just focused around recruitment. Having it be known as the ‘best place to find your next startup job’ is something I’d love to see.
